# Data

First-principles energies for molybdenum hydride/oxyhydride (MoOₘHₙ) species on
Cu(111) under alkaline HER conditions, from:

> Kambale, E. M.; Rivera Rocabado, D. S.; Kanematsu, Y.; Ishimoto, T.
> *Field-Dependent Redox Thermodynamics of MoOₘHₙ Species on Cu(111) and Ni(111)
> Surfaces under Alkaline Hydrogen Evolution Conditions.* Preprints.org, 2026.
> DOI: [10.20944/preprints202604.0944.v1](https://doi.org/10.20944/preprints202604.0944.v1)

The four adsorbates and their (m = waters, n = H) composition:

| Species   | m | n | character                |
|-----------|---|---|--------------------------|
| `H3Mo`    | 0 | 3 | reduced hydride          |
| `H3MoOH`  | 1 | 4 | hydride + hydroxyl       |
| `H2MoOH2` | 2 | 4 | di-hydroxyl              |
| `MoOOH3`  | 4 | 3 | oxidised oxyhydride      |

## `total_energy_vs_field.csv` — field scan (input to step 1)

DFT total energy (eV) of each system under a uniform applied field. One row per
system, one column per field value (`Field_-0.8` … `Field_0.8`, in V/Å). Includes
the `Cu(111)` slab and bare `Mo` reference alongside the four adsorbates; step 1
fits the adsorbates relative to `Mo`.

## `formation_terms_cu111.csv` — formation free-energy terms (input to step 2)

One column per species; rows are the terms entering the computational-hydrogen-
electrode free energy.

| Row        | Meaning                                          |
|------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| `G_system` | free energy of the adsorbate-covered surface (eV)|
| `G_Mo`     | free energy of the bare Mo reference (eV)         |
| `G_H2O`    | free energy of a water molecule (eV)              |
| `G_H2`     | free energy of H₂ (eV)                            |
| `m`        | water molecules exchanged                         |
| `n`        | hydrogen atoms                                    |

(The published energies tabulate these per species under opaque labels A–D; here
they are relabelled with the chemical species names. Mapping verified via (m, n):
A=H3Mo, B=H3MoOH, C=H2MoOH2, D=MoOOH3.)

## `field_response_fit.csv` — fitted dipole/polarizability (generated by step 1)

| Column    | Meaning                                                       |
|-----------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| `species` | adsorbate name                                                |
| `mu`      | surface dipole (slope of dG_rel vs field), relative to Mo      |
| `alpha`   | polarizability (curvature of dG_rel vs field), relative to Mo  |
| `R2`      | fit quality over the field window                              |
